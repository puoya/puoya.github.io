Hilbert manifold

Let \(\mathcal{H}\) be a Hilbert space. A Riemannian Hilbert manifold \((\mathcal{M}, g_p)\), Riemannian Hilbert is a smooth manifold modeled on the Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\), equipped with an inner product \(g_p\) on any tangent space \(T_p \mathcal{M}\) depending smoothly on \(p\) and defining on \(T_p \mathcal{M} \cong \mathcal{H}\) a norm equivalent to the one of \(\mathcal{H}\). Using \(g\), we can define the length of piecewise differential curve and it easy to check that for any two points of \(\mathcal{M}\) there exists a piecewise differential curve joining them. Hence, we can introduce a metric \(d\), defining \(d(x, y)\) the infimum of the lengths of all differential paths joining \(x, y\) and one can prove that (\(\mathcal{M}, d)\) is a metric space and d induces the same topology of \(\mathcal{M}\).
