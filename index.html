Research

My current research focus is quantitative finance. Previously, my work covered computational biology, group theory, differential geometry, machine learning, and statistical signal processing. Much of my work is motivated by fundamental questions in modeling, learning, and inference for complex systems.

Biography

I earned a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Amirkabir University of Technology (2014), and M.S. and Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) from Colorado State University (2016) and the University of Illinois at Urbana‑Champaign (2021), respectively. I then held postdoctoral positions in the Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute and ECE department at the University of California San Diego before transitioning to industry as a quantitative researcher at Capital Fund Management in Paris (2025-).
